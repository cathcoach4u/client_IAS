Accrual basis

IAS reports management figures on an accrual basis throughout the year. Revenue is recognised when clients are invoiced (not necessarily received) and expenses are recognised when invoices are raised (not necessarily paid). Year-end tax accounting is reported on a cash basis.

Global assumptions and allocation rules

  • Figures up to 30 April are year-to-date actuals for the 10 months ended 30 April.
  • Shared income is split evenly between General and Life only; Outsourcing receives no shared income.
  • Outsourcing allocation removes $50,000 from shared operating expenses before the remaining shared pool is split.
  • General and Life May + June costs are pro-rated from the 10-month YTD run-rate (YTD ÷ 10 × 2). Outsourcing May + June expenses use supplied figures.
  • Potential FY total = YTD actual + May + June estimate.
