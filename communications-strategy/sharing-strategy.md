# Sharing Strategy: Improving Communication & Protecting Your Position

## The Problem
Recent communication challenges have created some friction. The goal is to rebuild confidence through transparency, consistency, and demonstrable value.

---

## 5 Communication Habits to Implement Immediately

### 1. Fortnightly Progress Email to Jo (cc Leah)
**When:** Every second Monday
**Format:** 5-6 bullet points max

Template:
```
Subject: IAS AI Project - Fortnightly Update [Date]

Hi Jo,

Quick update on the AI project:

**Completed:**
- [bullet 1]
- [bullet 2]

**Next two weeks:**
- [bullet 1]
- [bullet 2]

**Any blockers:** [None / brief description]

Happy to discuss anything here.

Kind regards,
Cath
```

**Why this works:** Creates a paper trail, shows momentum, keeps Jo informed without requiring her time, and demonstrates accountability.

### 2. Session Agendas (Before Each Weekly Meeting)
**When:** 24 hours before each session with Leah/Lou
**Format:** 3 items max, sent via email or Teams

Template:
```
Agenda for [Date] Session:
1. [Topic - 20 mins]
2. [Topic - 20 mins]
3. [Topic - 20 mins]
```

**Why this works:** Shows preparation, keeps sessions focused, prevents scope drift.

### 3. Meeting Summaries (After Each Session)
**Who:** Leah sends the Teams meeting summary (she offered to do this)
**Your role:** Review the summary and reply with any corrections or additions within 24 hours

**Why this works:** Leah owns the process (as she wanted), you verify accuracy, creates shared record.

### 4. Value Log (Running Document)
**Where:** Shared document in SharePoint or OneNote (within IAS's Microsoft ecosystem)
**Format:** Simple table

| Date | What Was Done | Outcome/Benefit | Est. Time Saved |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 7 Apr | Set up Draft Instructions for 5 staff | Consistent email tone | ~15 min/person/week |
| 14 Apr | Created 5 prompt templates | Faster common email drafting | ~10 min/email |

**Why this works:** Builds the "value for money" case Jo wants to see at project end. Tangible, measurable, impossible to dispute.

### 5. Monthly Check-in with Jo
**When:** End of each month (30 mins max)
**Format:** Brief call or even a short email if a call isn't needed
**Content:** High-level progress, any strategic decisions needed, value delivered

**Why this works:** Maintains the direct relationship with the decision-maker, shows respect for her oversight role, prevents information being filtered.

---

## Key Principles for Protecting Your Position

1. **Stay in your lane.** Only discuss work within the 3 agreed buckets. Never introduce external products or tools unless asked.

2. **Let Microsoft be the hero.** Frame everything as "here's how to get more from what you already pay for." This positions you as a guide, not a vendor.

3. **Make Leah look good.** She's the project manager. Support her success and she'll advocate for your value.

4. **Document everything.** If it's not written down, it didn't happen. Use the value log, meeting summaries, and progress emails as your evidence base.

5. **Underpromise, overdeliver.** Set achievable goals each fortnight. Hitting them consistently rebuilds trust faster than ambitious promises.

6. **Be transparent about what you don't know.** If something is outside your expertise in the Microsoft ecosystem, say so and help them find the right resource. This builds credibility.
